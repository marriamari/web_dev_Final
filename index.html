Information of the Integration

Introduction

AI (Artificial Intelligence) is a contempt where computers have the ability to perform tasks that had previously or typically would require human intervention. These tasks include low scale, easier tasks to larger, more complex tasks. Things such as problem solving, reasoning, detailed explanations, and the ability to learn are all possible with AI.

ML (Machine Learning) is similar to AI but has a more specific task to further improve AI. ML develops different algorithms that allow the computer to perform tasks without human intervention at any point of the process, if needed. ML has the ability to learn over time like AI allowing it to do these actions.

Social media, web browsing, online shopping, applications, and self-driving cars all have AI integrated into their experiences. It is near impossible today to escape the influence of AI and ML in our daily activities. The curated content in ads, recommended videos, photos, music, destinations and more are all part of AI being used to guide the user in their online experience.

Ethics

There are many ethical situations to consider when interacting and using AI. From simple tasks for help on homework to integrating it into your own product these considerations should not go unnoticed no matter the scale. The biggest thing that AI aims to do is perform and expel answers as a human would. Giving a familiar response one would expect from a human.

All this to say, AI will provide the most human-like responses, even if that image is not real or that link is a fake link to a very real looking site. This can pose a dilemma where critical thinking and general knowledge of what AI is becomes so important.
